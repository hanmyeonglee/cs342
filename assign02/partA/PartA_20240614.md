## A-1. Probability

### Q1. Jackpot?

let $J\in\set{+j,-j}$ indicates whether the slot machine is jackpot mode or not, and $F\in\set{+f,-f}$ indicates whether it lights flash or not.  
Then the problem is calculating $P(J=+j\mid F=+f)$  

$P(J=+j)=0.025$, $P(F=+f\mid J=+j)=0.8$, $P(F=-f\mid J=-j)=0.1$ are given, then,

$1.\;P(J=+j\mid F=+f)=\dfrac{P(F=+f\mid J=+j)P(J=+j)}{P(F=+f)}$  
By `Beyes' Rule`

$2.\;P(F=+f)=P(F=+f\mid J=+j)P(J=+j)+P(F=+f\mid J=-j)P(J=-j)$  
By `Marginalization`

$\Rightarrow P(J=+j\mid F=+f)=\dfrac{P(F=+f\mid J=+j)P(J=+j)}{P(F=+f\mid J=+j)P(J=+j)+P(F=+f\mid J=-j)P(J=-j)}$

<span style="margin-right: 171px;"></span>$=\dfrac{0.8*0.025}{(1-0.1)*0.025+0.1*(1-0.025)}$

<span style="margin-right: 171px;"></span>$=\dfrac{1}{6}$

$\therefore P(J=+j\mid F=+f)=\dfrac{1}{6}$
